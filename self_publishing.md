**Gemini**

Moving from the technical grind of network tunnels to the creative landscape of self-publishing is a solid shift. Since you're crafting **hard science fiction**, you’ll appreciate that the "Kindred" approach to publishing is increasingly technical and data-driven.

Here is the roadmap for a self-published author in 2026:

### 1. The "Big Three" Distribution Hubs

You don't need a middleman to get into the major storefronts. You can manage these directly:

- **Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP):** The dominant force. It handles both ebooks and Print-on-Demand (POD) paperbacks.
- **IngramSpark:** Best for wide distribution. If you want your book available for libraries and local bookstores to order, this is the standard.
- **Draft2Digital:** An aggregator that pushes your book to Apple Books, Barnes & Noble, and international storefronts so you don't have to manage them individually.

### 2. Specialized Platforms for Hard Sci-Fi

Given your background in **Python** and **AI dev**, you might find these alternative paths interesting:

- **Serialized Platforms (Royal Road):** Huge for sci-fi and "LitRPG." You release chapters weekly to build a dedicated fanbase before the "official" book launch.
- **Direct Sales:** Since you're already building a **web-based audiobook platform** on GitHub Pages, you can sell digital copies directly to readers using tools like **Gumroad** or **Shopify**. This lets you keep \~95% of the profit instead of Amazon's 30–65%.

### 3. The Production Pipeline

As a **DAZ3D and ComfyUI user**, you have a massive advantage here. You can handle your own art assets:

- **Cover Design:** You can use your 3D modeling skills to create technically accurate hard sci-fi covers. Readers in this genre appreciate seeing "real" physics and ship designs.
- **Formatting:** Use tools like **Vellum** (Mac) or **Atticus** (Windows/Web) to turn your manuscript into professional EPUB and PDF files.
- **Narration:** You’ve already been experimenting with **F5-TTS** for narration. While many stores require a human narrator for "Audible" status, direct-sales fans often enjoy high-quality AI-assisted narration if it's disclosed.

### 4. ISBNs and Legalities

- **ISBNs:** In the US, you buy these from **Bowker**. You need a unique ISBN for every format (Ebook, Paperback, Hardcover).
- **Copyright:** Register your work with the **U.S. Copyright Office** once the final draft is locked.
- **Pricing:** Most sci-fi ebooks are priced between **$2.99 and $9.99**.

### 5. Building Your "Launch Pad"

While the book is still in development:

- **Mailing List:** This is an author's most valuable asset. Start a newsletter (using something like Substack or MailerLite) to collect emails from early readers.
- **ARC Readers:** Find "Advanced Review Copy" readers who will read the book for free in exchange for an honest review on launch day.

Since you are already using **AI to manage world-building** for *Echoes in the Fabric*, have you thought about how much of the "business" side you want to automate versus handling manually?

**Gemini**

Since you’re an **AI Dev** and **Python programmer**, you can treat the "business" of publishing like a series of automation pipelines rather than a sales pitch. You don't need "savvy" if you have a solid script.

Here is how to automate the business side of *Echoes in the Fabric*:

---

### 1. The Production Pipeline (The "Build" Stage)

Since you already use **DAZ3D**, **ComfyUI**, and **Python**, you can automate the visual and audio assets:

- **Cover Art Batching:** Use ComfyUI to generate consistent character and environment concept art for social media teasers and book covers.
- **Automated Audiobooks:** You’re already experimenting with **F5-TTS**. You can build a Python wrapper to batch-process your manuscript into high-fidelity audio chapters, which you are already hosting on your **GitHub Pages platform**.
- **Formatting with Markdown:** Write in Markdown and use **Pandoc** (via a Python script) to instantly convert your text into professional `.epub` (ebook) and `.pdf` (print) formats. No manual layout required.

### 2. The Distribution Pipeline (The "Deploy" Stage)

You want to avoid the "manual labor" of clicking through dozens of websites:

- **Aggregation Services:** Use **Draft2Digital**. You upload your book *once*, and their system automatically pushes it to Apple, Kobo, Barnes & Noble, and libraries worldwide. They handle the sales tax and international conversions for you.
- **Direct Sales Automation:** Use **Gumroad** or **LemonSqueezy**. They act as the "Merchant of Record," meaning they handle the global tax compliance (VAT, etc.) and file the paperwork. You just provide a link.

### 3. The Marketing Pipeline (The "Discovery" Stage)

This is where most authors fail, but it's just data management for you:

- **Newsletter Automation:** Set up **Substack** or **MailerLite**. You can create an "automation sequence" (a drip campaign). When someone joins your list, they automatically receive:
  1. A welcome email with a free short story.
  2. A "making of" email showing your **DAZ3D** renders.
  3. A link to buy the book.
- **AI Social Media Management:** Use tools like **Buffer** or **Metricool** to schedule months of sci-fi content at once. You can use your novel’s world-building data to generate "Lore Posts" automatically.

### 4. Legal & Admin (The "System Config")

- **Business Structure:** You don't need a fancy corporation yet. In the US, you can simply operate as a **Sole Proprietor** using your own name (or **Skot Xeno**) and your Social Security Number for tax purposes.
- **Bank Accounts:** Keep it simple. Use a separate free checking account (like **Mercury** or **Novo**) just for your writing income/expenses. It makes tax season a simple "Export to CSV" task.

---

### Your Technical Edge

Because you are an **A.I. Dev**, you can build a custom "World Bible" tool using a local LLM (like through **Ollama**) to keep your hard sci-fi physics and character arcs consistent. This prevents expensive "continuity" errors that usually require a human editor.

**The "Minimal Effort" Setup:**

1. **Draft2Digital** (for wide ebook sales).
2. **Amazon KDP** (for Print-on-Demand paperbacks).
3. **Substack** (for your fan list).
