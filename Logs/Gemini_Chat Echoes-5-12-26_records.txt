To disable smart quotes in Google Docs and use standard straight quotes instead, follow these steps:

- Open a document in Google Docs.
- Click on the Tools menu at the top.
- Select Preferences from the dropdown list.
- In the General tab, look for the checkbox labeled Use smart quotes.
- Uncheck that box and click OK.

Handling Existing Quotes
========================

Changing this setting only affects what you type moving forward. If you need to fix "curly" quotes that are already in your document:

- Press Ctrl + H (or Cmd + Shift + H on Mac) to open Find and Replace.
- In the Find box, type a quote mark.
- In the Replace with box, type the same quote mark.
- Click Replace all. Google Docs will replace the smart versions with straight ones.

For those working on a mobile device, this is often a function of your keyboard (like Gboard or the iOS keyboard) rather than the app itself. You may need to look into your phone's Keyboard Settings under Smart Punctuation.
