4. Heterna
  Defining Feature: Rigid Matriarchy, highly religious, specializing in advanced medicine and bio-synthetic textiles. 
  Scene Ideas for Conflict: 
  Conduit's Arrival: Conduit successfully arrives at the underground relay point near Heterna. But when the Heterna delegation comes to meet it, they send only a single, low-ranking young man, not a scientist or leader. This is an immediate, subtle insult to Veridia's status and the machine itself. 
  Dismissal of Veridia's Men: The subsequent video conference with Heterna's Matriarchate is fraught with tension. The Matriarch (maybe named The Elder or The Voice) refuses to address Corvin or Kael directly, only speaking to Varyn and Aris 
  Effect: Corvin, a man accustomed to authority, is reduced to a silent engineer. Kael, a brilliant scientist, is dismissed as a child. This tests their patience and their social expectations.
  Heterna is deeply suspicious of the machine-based Ionic network. They agree to share their vital medical and textile data, but only on the condition that all future communication and data processing for their city must be overseen by a female technician (Varyn or Aris) and no independent A.I. (Gus or Rixie) is allowed to handle their raw data. This is a functional demand based on prejudice.
  Heterna might not hate him because he's a robot, but because he is a Genderless, Soulless Machine doing a man's job (diplomacy and high-level tech). They might simply treat him as an inanimate, low-priority object. He sits in the underground relay station, waiting patiently, while the Matriarchate argues politics above him. This makes his loneliness and patience all the more poignant. 
  Heterna uses non-sentient robots as tools and slaves immediately sets them up as morally opposite to Veridia, where Vigilae (like Gus, Rixie, and Trindl) are considered members of the family and legal equals.
   Heterna's View: Machines are unthinking tools, without a soul, meant only for service. This difference will make the diplomacy incredibly tense. When the Matriarchs sees Veridia's lead scientist, Corvin, relying on Gus for calculations, or sees Kael teasing Rixie like a sister, they will be utterly appalled, viewing Veridia as having degraded their own social order by giving status to tools.
   The Conference: Dominated by Heterna's female leaders, who address only Varyn and Aris. Corvin and Kael are ignored or patronized.
   The Conflict: Heterna demands that Veridia's Vigilae be isolated from all Heterna data, revealing their core belief that A.I. cannot be trusted or given status.
  
  The Trade-off: The shared medical/textile data is too valuable to lose, forcing Veridia's team to compromise their pride and values to secure Heterna's knowledge for the benefit of all Dorin.
  This gives the chapter high stakes without high body counts. It's a test of diplomacy, patience, and Kael's ability to communicate his brilliant ideas when his authority is stripped away.
  Provides its people with extremely long life: Hundreds of years.
  Also have rigid and strict control over children, whom are grown in tanks and not born normally. Pregnancy is not even possible naturally.
