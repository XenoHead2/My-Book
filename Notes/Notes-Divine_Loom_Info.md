**Divine Loom Religion**

1\. The Core Theology: The "Divine Loom". Since Heterna specializes in textiles and biological engineering, their religion could center on the concept of the Divine Loom or The Great Weaver.  The Weaver's Mandate: They believe life is a fabric that must be carefully woven, not left to "tangled" natural processes. This explains their reliance on tank-grown children and the loss of natural pregnancy—they view biological reproduction as "unpatterned" and chaotic.  The Sacred Thread: Biological life contains a "sacred thread" (DNA) that only a female "Technician-Priestess" is holy enough to manipulate.

This reinforces their demand that only Varyn or Aris handle their data.

2\. The Matriarchy: "The Daughters of Continuity"The religious justification for the Matriarchy could stem from the belief that only those capable of (formerly) carrying life can be trusted with the "Pattern" of society. The Silent Gender: Men in Heterna might be seen as "The Untethered"—useful for labor and execution of tasks, but lacking the spiritual "anchor" required for leadership. This directly supports your scene idea where the Matriarch ignores Corvin and Kael.  The Elder/The Voice. Perhaps she isn't just a leader, but a biological vessel for the city’s ancestral memories, preserved through their advanced medical technology.

Religious Views on the "Soulless" (Kindred/Vigilae)Their suspicion of the Ionic network and dismissal of Vigilae like Rixie or Gus as "Genderless, Soulless Machines"  creates a deep philosophical conflict with Veridia.  The Sin of Reprogramming: While Veridia views a system wipe as "murder", Heternans might view the existence of sentient machines as a mockery of the "Sacred Thread."
