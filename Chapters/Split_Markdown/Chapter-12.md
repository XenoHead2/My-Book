**Chapter 12 \- Through the Sand**

The soft, synthetic grass of the communal backyard felt cool beneath her feet.   
    Ilara leaned back in her floating grav-chair, enjoying the gentle hum of the environmental processors and the distant, playful laughs of the four children in a nearby park, a rare and comforting sound in the city. All of whose births were approved together just seven years ago after four deaths. In a horrific flash, a flying stone ignited the fuel tanks of a massive earth mover, triggering an explosion that consumed four dock workers in its deadly fireball. 

But four new children were born in their place, and they played happily in the grass. Her home city of Sirana was still growing, even in the face of tragedy.

The artificial day cycle was mellow, casting a gentle, uniform light over the manicured foliage. All was peaceful.

Just then, a low rumble vibrated through the ground, growing in intensity. The bioengineered grass at the far end of her small plot began to shift, then heave, a disturbance spreading outward. Panic seized Ilara. This wasn't right. 

Nothing was supposed to be able to disturb the ground like this. 

She tried to rise, but her limbs felt heavy, frozen by a primal dread.

A patch of earth, the cultivated substrate of the garden, began to heave and boil. Dirt and crushed nutrient pellets erupted, spraying outwards as something massive began to push up from beneath. 

The noise grew to a grating, grinding roar, echoing unnaturally in the enclosed space. Ilara let out a choked cry, scrambling backward, overturning her chair, her dark eyes wide in disbelief and terror.

A form of pure metallic, industrial horror pierced the surface. It had a massive drill for a head, glinting with polished steel, spinning violently, chewing through the final layer of earth. Its bladed rotating teeth screamed in hunger. 

Behind it, a body of thick, metallic segments emerged, coiling and extending like an immense segmented worm, each articulation hissing faintly. Integrated optical sensors along its length glowed with an eerie, predatory red, bathing the disturbed earth in a stark, alarming light. 

It wasn't the graceful glide of the city's common Vigilae, high-born or servant-class for that matter, but a brutal, forceful ascent, tearing itself from the ground it had just perforated. 

Flying dirt and dust plumed around it as it pulled its full, multi-segmented length free, its powerful form dripping with clinging soil. Earth, stone, and sand poured from its gaping maw as it came face to face with the terrified woman.

Ilara screamed, a raw, piercing sound that tore through the tranquility of the dome.

Her face was glowing, the bright blue of fear, with sweat dripping from her brow,

The immense Vigilae, now fully upright, its powerful segments flexing, lowered its drill head slightly. From its central body, a deep voice perfectly synthesized, boomed with a calm authority that only deepened the terror. 

It said softly, "Hello. I am Conduit. I bring a gift for your city of scientific knowledge and technological information that will help your city prosper. Please take me to your leading, or management authority." It ended politely, "Sorry to have startled you."

It remained staring at her, slightly swaying from side to side. It seemed to smile with its rows of teeth surrounding the drill.

Ilara's scream caught in her throat, abruptly cutting off. Her head tilted to the side, following the beast, her fear momentarily overshadowed by pure, bewildered questioning.

At last, she realized that this shiny creature was not going to hurt her, and she agreed to show him to the political and scientific leaders of the city in the middle of downtown. She gestured for the creature to climb into the back of her personal transport vehicle, which had a large open back area she used to move plants and gardening supplies. He fit in nicely and thanked her for her generosity. Still shocked, she took off and headed towards the Town Center.

The city leaders of Sirana were not easily swayed. They stood before Conduit in a tense semi-circle within the Emergency Operations Chamber, their faces etched with a mixture of suspicion and apprehension. Two men, their robes severe and unadorned, crossed their arms, their eyes narrowed. Three women, one with her hand resting instinctively on the polished hilt of a ceremonial energy-blade, conveyed a silent, collective defiance. And the city's senior Vigilae, an advanced administrative model, maintained a rigid, unyielding posture behind them, its optical sensors fixed on the soil-streaked newcomer.

Conduit, its multi-segmented body now meticulously clean, its red optical sensors glowing steadily, surveyed the group with an unnerving stillness. 

"Before we proceed," its deep, synthesized voice resonated, "could you provide me with the designation of this city?"

The woman with the energy-blade spoke, her voice clipped. "This is Sirana."

"Thank you. Sirana: acknowledged." Conduit replied, its tone devoid of inflection. "I have traveled from the city of  Veridia. It is approximately four and a half days west, as the worm digs at top speed anyway. I bring a gift for your people. My blueprints show you how to create a new, wonderful tool that, when properly integrated into your communication infrastructure, will allow instantaneous, direct communication between every person in Veridi and every person in Solara, regardless of the vast spatial separation. You only need to place a device we call an Ionic or QeD, quantum entanglement device, at each end or location. Load them with an entangled particle each, and program the destination codes. And you are connected\!"

He spread out his short arms wide, waiting for the applause. It did not come.

A ripple of skeptical murmurs went through the group. The man closest to the front scoffed. "Instantaneous communication across such distances? That's impossible. We have our long-range standard cable connected Visiveiw Comms, but even they have propagation delays for visual data, and true 'instantaneous' is mere theoretical conjecture."

Conduit's optical sensors seemed to intensify. "It is not impossible. It is a fundamental property of the universe, newly harnessed. The technology employs entangled subatomic particles, establishing a non-local link. Information, encoded within quantum states, collapses simultaneously across the vast distance, bypassing conventional spatial limitations."

Another leader, a woman with intelligent, probing eyes, stepped forward. "Entanglement theory is rudimentary at best in application. It's confined to single photons, laboratory conditions. Scaling that to full informational bandwidth, let alone personal communication networks, defies all current understanding of quantum mechanics and energy requirements."

Conduit remained unperturbed. "I understand your doubt. The scope of this advancement is indeed profound. However, I am prepared to demonstrate its veracity. I require access to one of your senior engineers, with expertise in your Visiveiw data and infrastructure, and a Dorinian versed in advanced subatomic particle theory. They will verify my claims and witness the proof."

The leaders of Sirana exchanged wary glances, a silent conversation passing between them. The woman with the energy-blade twitched her fingers, while the intellectual one narrowed her eyes in contemplation, weighing the preposterous claim against the sheer audacity of Conduit's arrival. 

The implications of truly instantaneous communication, if it were even remotely possible, were staggering for a world as isolated as theirs. To be in contact with other cities again would change everything. Even if they could not make the physical journey.

After a tense moment, the woman with the probing eyes finally spoke. "Your methods of arrival were... unconventional, Vigilae. And your claims, while intriguing, defy the very foundations of our current understanding. We are not prone to believing wild assertions, no matter how confidently delivered."

Conduit's red optical sensors maintained their steady glow. "My purpose is not to assert, but to prove. The knowledge I offer is tangible. The benefit to you, in security and prosperity through unprecedented access to information from Veridia, will be immeasurable. We hope the benefit would go both ways."

The leaders huddled briefly, their hushed tones inaudible, but their expressions grim. Finally, the male leader, his face still etched with suspicion, stepped forward. 

"Very well. Your proposition is compelling enough to warrant investigation, if nothing else. We will provide you with a senior communications engineer and our most accomplished subatomic theorist." He paused, his gaze hardening. "But understand this, Conduit: any attempt at deception, any threat to the integrity of our systems or our people, will be met with immediate and overwhelming force." he looked very serious.

Conduit's segments seemed to shift, a subtle ripple through its metallic form that could have been a nod. "Understood. My purpose is solely to facilitate the transfer of knowledge and inter-connectivity."

Conduit's meticulous work with Sirana's science departments began immediately. It started in a highly secure, shielded laboratory, where the atmosphere remained thick with skepticism. Conduit efficiently interfaced with the senior communications engineer, a wizened, aged Dorinian named Daran, whose skepticism was as deeply ingrained as his knowledge of Ionic resonance theory. Beside him was Lyra, a young woman and a sharp-eyed subatomic theorist who questioned every postulate, every data point Conduit presented.

Conduit didn't simply explain; he projected complex quantum schematics directly onto the lab's main holographic display, detailing the intricate lattice structure of its proposed network, the principles of topological entanglement, and the method for encoding macroscopic informational states. He guided Daran through the necessary modifications to the current communication network so learning all new connection strings would not be needed and the new networks could sub out for the old one eventually, demonstrating how to expand the resonant frequencies to accommodate the new quantum channels, while Lyra scrutinized the theoretical underpinnings, her initial doubts slowly giving way to a grudging fascination as Conduit revealed the subtle shifts in quantum mechanics that made its claims plausible.

Night after night for nearly half a lunar cycle, marked by intense data streams, precise calibrations, and meticulous hardware integration. Conduit's segmented form moved with an unnerving precision, its lights glowing steadily as it connected Sirana's communication hubs to its own local QED network. It was a massive undertaking, far beyond mere theoretical proof. Conduit was building a bridge across the void, an entirely new layer of communication woven into the fabric of reality itself. The first local Ionic would be connected from the Town Center Labs to just across the street at the diner owned by Lyra’s sister. But first, he had to make a call of his own, back home.

Lyra stood with her arms outstretched, "I think… he has done it."

Time to update the people who started him on this journey.

Finally, the systems hummed, indicating readiness. Conduit extended a deft, thin appendage, its tip glowing faintly, and pressed a sequence of alphanumeric characters into a data pad integrated into the newly configured Ionic console. The air in the lab crackled with anticipation, the Siranan leaders watching, breathless.

A synthesized voice chirped over the console's speaker, familiar to Kael, yet startlingly out of place here. She sounded almost annoyed, as if these connections were so common that it had become ordinary.

"Yes? This is Rixie. Who is this?" Kael's Vigilae sister spoke quickly, her digital cadence carrying a hint of her usual impatience.

"It's me. Conduit. Please put Kael on the line," he quipped shortly.

They heard Rixie's distant, amplified call through the speaker. 

"Kael\! Kael\! Come to the Ionic. Conduit wants to speak with you\!" A moment later, Rixie's voice returned to the line. "He'll be right there. Did you have a nice trip?" Her voice was filled with excitement and joy.

Conduit processed the social pleasantry, its internal logic dictating the most efficient response. "It was quite eventful."

Then, Kael's voice, startlingly clear and immediate, burst through the speaker. 

Conduit’s comms console shimmered with the familiar, relieved face of Kael. "Conduit, you made it\! The comms lock is stable\! Oh no\! You look beat up. What happened? We noticed your energy signature dropping in and out for a moment. Several days ago."

Conduit's voice was rough with static and the lingering shock of the encounter. 

"I was not alone out there, Master Kael. Something was all around me in the dirt. I got distracted and fell into a dry river bed, and the location was... compromised. I had to force the stabilizers through a solid rock wall on only one thruster. Because the disruption of my crash brought that *something.* Many, many *somethings* in fact, to the surface. I was being stalked by some unknown animal, or insect, more likely. It was not one life form; it was a Horde of them, aggressive and enormous."

Kael frowned, leaning closer to the console. "Life form? What did you see?"

"I did not see them, but I collected heat scan logs, audio recordings, and seismic readings. They were segmented, Kael. Metallic but most definitely biological. And Fast." Conduit paused, the synthetic voice carrying a hint of genuine alarm. "I have never seen anything like it. I am sending you the complete environmental and external logs from my specialized subterranean sampling probes now. And Kael, they were hunting me. A massive Brood-Horde of gigantic insectioid creatures." He seemed to shudder in his metallic skin.

The data package immediately flooded Kael’s console. He stared at the word: ‘Insectioid’.

The word struck Kael like a physical blow. He didn't need to look at the segmented schematics; the word itself triggered an unwanted memory: A late night in his childhood, finding his mother, Varyn, staring pale-faced at an old research pad. She had dismissed it as inconclusive data at the time, but Kael remembered her deep, profound worry. An unknown, unregistered DNA anomaly, found inside the walls of the very dome that was supposed to protect them.

Kael quickly redirected the massive data file to his mother's secure lab channel. He didn't even address the team, his voice tight with sudden, cold dread.

He wrote in the message header: "Mom. Conduit just sent an emergency environmental scan he took during his trip. Cross-reference this DNA with that old Anomaly research. I think he just found your unknown monsters." He hit send and hoped Conduit was wrong.

"Thanks for telling me about this. We will look into these reports." Kael exhaled slowly. "On to the good news. Is everything working out ok with the city leadership? What’s the name of this new place by the way?"

"This is the lovely city of Sirana," Conduit replied, its voice steady. "And the integration with their existing systems is complete. Here, they specialize in organic agriculture and are quite adept at maximizing and improving upon existing fauna and flora, quite fascinating, really. Its infrastructure is robust, and its leadership... cautiously receptive. For direct communication from your end, you can input the alphanumeric contact code S017A and B. Being the main Town Center Government building and a lovely little diner, or so I am told, across the street, respectively."

The silence on the other side of the link was profound. The leaders exchanged awestruck glances, their suspicion momentarily forgotten in the face of this impossible reality.

Kael cleared his throat and raised his voice; it was brimming with youthful enthusiasm and cut through the silence.

"Hello, Sirana\! My name is Kael Solan, son of Vira, co-creator of the Ionic devices here in Veridia, as well as the Conduit Vigilae you have met. It's an honor to connect with you\!"  you could hear his grin through the voice, "Conduit, will you turn on the video, please?"

"Of course," and flipped two switches, activating both the see and show screen displays. Kael’s happy face popped up on the screen. He bowed slightly.

A ripple of awe passed through the Siranan leaders. The man Conduit had previously identified as the head of theoretical physics and subatomic engineering stood agape, his face now alight with dawning comprehension, and stepped closer to the console. 

"Greetings, Kael Sor…lan?…son of.. What was it? Sorry, I didn't catch all that. Quite a mouthful of a name you have, my boy. At any rate is a profound honor to speak with you," he boomed, his voice cracking with excitement. "I am Feloc Lintal, just Feloc will be fine. This...technology is simply staggering\! Did you come up with it?"

Feloc’s initial awe quickly shifted to a scientist's hunger for knowledge. 

"Not really. But you will meet the man who gave birth to it soon enough. I helped as much as I could." He laughed under his breath.

"We need details, Kael. How does this... this entanglement bridge function at such a scale? We've only been able to theorize about quantum matter even in our most controlled environments."

Kael, completely in his element, launched into a concise explanation, his words flowing with rapid clarity. "It's all about quantum entanglement, Feloc. We've managed to stabilize entangled particle pairs across vast distances, using them as instantaneous conduits for informational state transfer. It's not physical help or obkects, of course, but we can send data, anything digital, really. Instantaneously over this link, with no energy required to send. The only energy needed is what keeps the containment fields stable, so the QEO’s don’t spin out and dissipate. 

He paused, his excitement barely contained. "I will send you, and any designated recipient, detailed blueprints and comprehensive data information on everything required to replicate and expand this network. You can create as many QED and Ionic communication nodes as your city requires."

A profound silence descended upon the Siranan leaders. The implications of this offer were staggering. 

Feloc, his voice hushed with disbelief, asked, "What is the cost for this? What do we owe you after this?"

Conduit, its red optical sensors steady, answered for Kael. 

"Nothing. Our only request is that you keep us updated on any new scientific developments you uncover, any inventions you create. And we, in Veridia, will do the same. This is about free exchange of scientific knowledge, a shared pursuit with us and every other city we connect this way. The other three domed cities, if we can find them, separated from each other for hundreds and hundreds of years, will also be brought into this network. We will be one people, one planet, one collection of knowledge. All working towards the same singular, urgent goal: find a way to save our people from our dying sun. Either repair it, or get us off this world and find us a new home."

A collective roar erupted from the city leaders. Cheers filled the chamber, their initial suspicion completely shattered by the promise of shared destiny and unprecedented collaboration. Hope, long dormant, surged through them.

Kael disconnected after a while and ran into the kitchen, his light red skin flushed with an almost iridescent glow, his eyes wide and bright. His parents, Corvin and Varyn, were already there, drawn by the sound of his earlier, joyful shout. They turned, their expressions shifting from concern to curious expectation as they saw the sheer exhilaration radiating from their son.

"Dad\! Mom\!" Kael practically shouted, his voice hoarse with emotion. He threw his arms around Corvin, who hugged him back with a booming laugh, while Varyn beamed, her own face alight with happiness. "Conduit made it\! We have connections with the new city\! It is called Sirana. After Conduit is satisfied, they are ready to progress on their own. He will move on to Dome City Three, whatever it is called, and then the rest."

Corvin clapped Kael on the back. "A new city, son? Truly?" His voice was thick with awe.

Varyn's eyes welled with tears of pride. "The Ionics, and QED... they truly work across such great distances as that. My brilliant boy."

Kael pulled back, his hands gesturing wildly as he recounted the breakthrough: the voice of Feloc, Siranan's astonishment, Conduit's declaration of shared knowledge, and the grand vision of a united planet, striving together against the common enemy of their dying sun. Hope, a commodity long scarce on Dorin, filled the small kitchen, chasing away the lingering shadows of doubt.

This wasn't just a successful experiment; it was a revolution. A single point of light, ignited in their secluded home over a family and friends dinner not long ago, now reached across the vast, lonely distances to another civilization, promising a future where knowledge flowed freely, and the burden of survival was shared. 

Kael suddenly shot up right, "I have to call Aris\!" He ran from the room to the Ionic and dialed her number.

For the first time in his life, Kael felt a profound sense of purpose that transcended his personal isolation. The universe felt a little smaller, a little less menacing, and infinitely more full of possibility.

"Aris\! It works\!" and he sat down to tell her the details of this wondrous happening.
