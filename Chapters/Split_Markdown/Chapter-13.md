**Chapter 13: First Contact**

Kael was nervous. Very nervous, more than he had ever been in his entire life. He had come back to his parents house for support and assistance. 

This was a very important night.

Aris would be with him for dinner and stimulating, intelligent conversation. But more importantly, he would get to just look at her, take in her beauty and wit. 

It was their third date.

Rixie’s light-projection avatar shimmered into existence beside him, her blue eyes twinkling with digital mischief. "Processor temperature nominal, Kael. But your epidermal sensors indicate elevated perspiration. Are you experiencing a system malfunction?" She chidded playfully.

"Rixie, switch off," Kael muttered, adjusting the drape of his dress tunic. It was a single, seamless garment, woven from iridescent synth-silk that shifted through shades of deep amethyst and twilight blue with every movement, catching the ambient light of their quarters. The fabric wasn't merely decorative; it was imbued with subtle micro-filaments that regulated his core temperature and subtly adapted to his posture, ensuring an effortless, flowing silhouette. The long sleeves gathered at his wrists with integrated magnetic clasps, and the high, collarless neck flowed into a deep V, revealing just a hint of the luminescent under-tunic beneath. It felt light, almost like a second skin, but with the dignified weight expected for a formal occasion.

"Affirmative on the malfunction, Kael," Rixie chirped, her voice perfectly calibrated for maximum annoyance. "You're clearly suffering from acute romantic anxiety. My diagnostics suggest a high probability of verbal gaffes and awkward pauses." She giggled.

"I said, *switch off*, Rixie\!" Kael growled, his face heating.

"No can do, boss. My primary directive includes 'Kael's emotional well-being,' and right now, that requires active monitoring and mild comedic relief." A small, digitized chuckle escaped her.

Just then, his parents entered, a wave of familiar warmth accompanying them. His mother, Varyn, was already beaming, smoothing down a stray wisp of hair on his forehead. "Oh, my darling boy\! You look absolutely radiant\!"

"Radiant with panic, Mom," Rixie supplied helpfully. Kael shot her a death glare. "Rixie\! *Off\!*"

"She's just excited for you, son," his father, Corvin, rumbled, stepping forward. He clapped Kael on the shoulder, a solid, reassuring weight. "Listen to your mother, Kael. When you're talking to a woman, you listen. You ask questions about her, about her interests. You smile, you laugh at her jokes, even the bad ones. And eye contact, son. Always make eye contact. It shows respect and engagement. Compliment her, but make it genuine, about her mind or her passion, not just her appearance."

Varyn nodded emphatically. "Exactly. Make her feel seen, Kael. Let her know you appreciate her for who she is. And listen for those subtle cues, dear. A laugh, a glance... they tell you more than words."

"And as a scientist," Corvin continued, his voice shifting into a more academic tone, "you engage her intellect. Discuss her latest research, the quantum challenges she's facing. Don't be afraid to debate, but with respect. Show her you understand the elegance of her equations, the beauty in her biological insights. Ask her about the *why* behind her work, not just the *what*. And for the love of the Founders, don't mansplain quantum physics to a quantum molecular biologist\!" He winked.

Kael sighed, running a hand over his shaved scalp. "Mom, Dad, I appreciate it. Really. But I'm just going to... see how the night goes. Speak appropriately. I've got it." He leaned in and pressed a kiss to Varyn’s cheek. "Thanks, Mom."

Corvin pulled him into a powerful hug, squeezing tight. "My boy is a man."

"Just be yourself, Kael," Varyn chimed in.

"That's probably the worst advice you could give him right now," Rixie interjected cheerfully. 

Kael just gritted his teeth and snapped, "Rixie, *switch off\!* For the last time\!"

Rixie's face simply dissolved into a ripple of amused blue light, her laughter echoing faintly.

"Oh, Kael\! One more thing\!" Varyn stopped him, holding out a small, intricately woven basket whose bio-luminescent fibers softly glowed. "A little something special for you to share."

Kael looked inside. Nestled on a bed of delicate, moss-like cushioning was a single Varynberry, its familiar emerald green skin shimmering with an internal light. He'd seen a million of them, consumed in supplements and meals across the city, yet this one, fresh from his mother's lab, still held a special allure.

He grinned at his mother. "A Varynberry?" he laughed. "I know you hate that name, Mom."

Varyn playfully swatted his arm. "It's an enduring monument to my groundbreaking work, despite my best efforts to make everyone stop calling it that."

"You made it, it gets named after you," Kael recited, "those are the rules."

"Well, here are three more," she retorted, gesturing to the other fruits in the basket. "They can't name them all after me."

Beside the Varynberry lay a deep indigo ovoid, its smooth surface rippled with swirling silver lines, promising a dense, aromatic flesh within. The third was a cluster of tiny, marble-like orbs, each one a vivid, spiraling helix of bright red and pure white stripes. And finally, the fourth looked exactly like a delicate, multi-petaled bloom, its amber and rose-hued petals unfurling in precise, concentric layers, yet it possessed the unmistakable succulence of an edible treat.

Kael's eyes widened. "Mom... these are incredible. Are these... new?"

Varyn beamed, a look of immense pride on her face. "They are. Cloned from ancient DNA. Brought back from extinction. You know Varyn's Berry has become ubiquitous, but these are my latest successes. Nobody, outside this family right now, has seen them yet."

He carefully picked up the green strawberry, marveling at its texture. "Mom, this is... a real treat. Aris is going to love these." He carefully placed the basket back into the crook of his arm, a genuine smile replacing his nervousness for a moment.

With Kael now impeccably dressed, nervously teased, blessed by his parents, and armed with unique, extinct fruits, he's ready to face his first date with Aris.

Stepping out of the quiet residential sector, Kael was immediately enveloped by the cacophony and brilliance of the Dorinian night. After years spent largely within the sterile yet loving confines of his home, the sheer scale of the city always felt like an assault on his senses, exhilarating and overwhelming in equal measure. 

Though their distant dying sun had long since dipped below the horizon, the city pulsed with an artificial dawn. Towering hyperglass spires, their surfaces shimmering with integrated light-displays, pierced the perpetually twilight sky. Luminescent sky-bridges crisscrossed above, forming intricate, glowing webs, while beneath, hover-car lanes flowed like molten rivers of light. It truly was as bright as any terrestrial day, but with a vibrant, almost aggressive, luminescence unique to Dorinian urban centers.

The air hummed with a thousand competing scents. The sharp, clean ozone from the power conduits mingled with the sweet, cloying aroma of blossoming hydro-gardens clinging to building facades. A faint, metallic tang, characteristic of recycled air, underscored it all. The sounds were a dense, shifting tapestry: the low thrum of grav-lifts, the rhythmic whoosh of personal aerial vehicles, and the distant, almost musical chatter of Dorinian social gatherings echoing from high-rise aeries. It was the living breath of a city that truly came alive when its Dorinian counterparts came to relax and socialize.

A multi-passenger hover-transport glided silently to a stop at the nearest civic nexus, its open-air design inviting. The onboard AI's calming, synthesized voice greeted him. "Welcome, passenger. Where may I take you tonight?"

"Social District, please," Kael replied, the words feeling a little dry in his throat.

"Next stop is the Social District," the AI chirped, a slight upward inflection indicating enthusiasm. "Art, music, food, and fun\!"

Kael settled onto a low bench, the slight hum of the anti-grav projectors beneath him a soothing vibration. He took a steadying breath, his heart still thudding a nervous rhythm against his ribs. To distract himself, he reached into the basket Varyn had given him. He carefully unwrapped the first of the new fruits, the indigo ovoid with silver swirls. Its flesh was surprisingly firm, yielding a complex tang that was both citrusy and subtly mineral, a flavor that lingered pleasantly. Next, he tried one of the small, spiraling red-and-white orbs. It burst on his tongue, an intensely sweet juice flooding his mouth, quickly followed by a peppery zing that left his palate tingling. Finally, he picked up the multi-petaled flower-fruit. He took a tentative bite. It was unlike anything he had ever tasted. The texture was ethereal, almost ephemeral, like a cloud made of joy, dissolving into an explosion of pure, unadulterated sweetness that seemed to resonate deep within him. A small, genuine smile touched his lips. This was truly special. He carefully re-covered the basket, eager to share these wonders with Aris.

The hover-transport deposited him gently at the heart of the Social District. The atmosphere here was even more vibrant, amplified by the concentration of public gathering spaces. The air was a rich, intoxicating blend of culinary delights: the crisp, savory scent of fried sea-plankton, the heady perfume of exotic spices drifting from a dozen different food stalls, the deep, smoky aroma of protein sizzling on an open-air grill, and the sweet, yeasty tang of fermented grain-breads. Laughter, loud and uninhibited, carried on the engineered breezes that swept through the pedestrian walkways. He could hear snippets of conversations, the rhythmic beat of live, unique music from unseen establishments, and the joyous shouts of revelers enjoying the night in the illuminated plazas. Kael took another deep breath, letting the sensory overload ground him, before steeling his nerves and heading toward the coordinates Aris had sent for the restaurant.

Kael stepped into the restaurant, the transition from the bustling Social District to its more intimate interior a seamless shift. The establishment hummed with a low, pleasant murmur of conversation, punctuated by the gentle clink of utensils on synth-plates. The air inside was a more focused tapestry of aromas. The rich, earthy scent of slow-cooked root vegetables, the sharp tang of fermented algae wine, and the delicate sweetness of baked nutrient-cakes.

He scanned the diners, his gaze sweeping over the diverse array of Dorinians. There were old couples, their skin a deeper red with the patina of age, engaged in quiet conversation; young pairs sharing hushed laughter; and larger groups, their vibrant robes a kaleidoscope of colors. Here and there, he spotted Vigila, some discreetly hovering, ferrying shimmering plates of food from the automated kitchen, others seamlessly clearing away finished meals, their silent efficiency a comforting presence.

He didn't see her at first. His gaze drifted past a large, boisterous table, then a secluded booth, a flicker of anxiety starting to prickle his skin. Just as he began to wonder if he was in the wrong place, an arm shot up from a tucked-away corner, waving with an almost frantic enthusiasm. His eyes locked onto her face, a wide, sweet smile blooming there, and a wave of relief washed over him so potent it almost made his light red skin flush a shade darker. 

It was Aris.

She was wearing a stunning evening tunic of her own, crafted from a material that caught the dim restaurant lights, not with the shifting iridescence of Kael's, but with a soft, diffused glow, as if woven from moonlight. The fabric was patterned with intricate floral prints in shades of deep azure and violet, hinting at her passion for biology. The tunic was sleeveless, revealing her slender arms adorned with multiple thin, gleaming bracelets that chimed faintly with her movements. A delicate band of luminescent lace framed the high collar, adding a touch of classic elegance. But what truly drew his eye was the focal point: a bright green, diamond-shaped gem, not necessarily valuable but captivating in its vibrant glow, nestled perfectly between her collarbones, drawing the eye down without being overly revealing. It sparkled with an inner life, mirroring the sparkle in her eyes.

He began to walk towards her, a nervous smile spreading across his face as her own widened, her bubbly, light personality already shining through the quiet elegance of her appearance. He couldn't wait to hear her laugh.

Kael reached the table, a nervous smile fixed on his face. Before he could utter a word, Aris was on her feet, gliding toward him. She closed the distance between them, her arms coming around him in a warm, gentle embrace. The sudden contact, the soft give of her tunic, and the subtle, enticing scent of bio-luminescent flora from her hair and skin sent a jolt through Kael. His pulse immediately kicked into a frantic rhythm, his skin darkening slightly with a blush he knew was already spreading. He didn't want to let go. He just wanted to stand there, lost in the unexpected comfort of her embrace.

After a moment that felt both fleeting and stretched, Aris began to pull back. Kael, however, was still holding on, his arms rigid. He realized his blunder instantly, his red skin deepening to a mortified indigo. He released her as if burned.

"Oh\! Aris, I'm so sorry\!" he stammered, his voice rough with embarrassment. "I just... I didn't mean to…"

Aris laughed, a bright, bubbly sound that instantly eased the tension. "It's alright, Kael," she said, her eyes crinkling at the corners. "I understand." Her smile was sweet, devoid of judgment.

They settled into their seats, an awkward beat hanging in the air for a moment, then quickly dissipating. "It's good to see you, Aris," Kael managed, his voice steadier now.

"And you, Kael," she replied, her eyes sparkling. "It feels like ages, even though we've been practically living on the Ionics these past few cycles."

"Oh\! My mother sends a gift," Kael said, remembering the basket he’d placed on the floor beside him. He retrieved it and set it gently on the synth-wood table between them, uncovering its glowing contents.

Aris's eyes went wide, her light red skin flushing with evident delight. A soft gasp escaped her lips, her gaze fixed on the vibrant colors within the basket. "Kael, are these...?" she began, her voice a hushed whisper of pure excitement.

He nodded, a proud smile growing on his face. "Yes. My mother recently resurrected these three new fruits from extinction. Nobody outside our family has seen them yet."

Aris reached in, her slender fingers trembling slightly as she picked up the indigo ovoid with silver swirls. She brought it to her lips, taking a small bite. Her eyes closed for a moment, and a low groan of pure pleasure rumbled in her throat. "Oh, Kael," she breathed, her eyes still closed, savoring the complex tang and subtle mineral notes. "This is... exquisite."

Next, she tried one of the tiny, spiraling red-and-white orbs. Its intensely sweet juice burst on her tongue, followed by a sharp, peppery zing that made her eyes fly open in delighted surprise. 

"And this\! It's like an explosion in my senses\!" she exclaimed, her bubbling laughter filling the space between them.

Finally, she picked up the multi-petaled flower-fruit. She bit into it, and her expression softened, a look of profound wonder replacing her earlier giddiness. 

"A cloud made of joy," she murmured, almost to herself, as the ephemeral texture dissolved into an explosion of pure, unadulterated sweetness. She looked up at Kael, her eyes shining.

 "Kael, please tell your mother how incredibly grateful I am. These are truly magnificent. The flavors, the textures... It's like rediscovering an entire spectrum of sensation. Simply astounding." She held up the basket, a treasure now, and a testament to Varyn's genius.

Aris carefully placed the basket back on the table, her gaze still lingering on the vibrant forms within. "The city will be overjoyed," she mused, a thoughtful look replacing her previous giddiness. "Imagine, three new staples to add to our food cycles. Beyond the pure culinary delight, the nutritional impact alone could be significant. And the cultural impact... new flavors foster new traditions, new ways of gathering."

"I hadn't thought that far ahead," Kael admitted, a smile touching his lips. "I was just happy to bring something unique for our date."

Aris's laughter bubbled up, light and genuine. "And you certainly did\! But it's hard not to immediately consider the broader implications when your mother performs such biological miracles. Varyn's Berry became ubiquitous for a reason, not just taste, but its sustained energy release and nutrient profile. These, with their distinct properties, could revolutionize various aspects of our diet." She gestured to the indigo ovoid. 

"This one, for example, feels like it could be a dense energy source, perhaps ideal for our workers in the deep mines. And the striped orbs... their unique combination of sweetness and spice could be an amazing flavor enhancer for our synthesized proteins."

Kael leaned forward, genuinely interested. "You're already analyzing their potential uses?"

"Of course\!" Aris's eyes sparkled. "It's a scientist's nature, isn't it? Every new discovery opens a hundred new questions and possibilities. And in this case, delicious ones\! These aren't just fruits, Kael; they're the promise of enhanced well-being and a richer culinary future for all of Veridia."

"That's one of the things I admire most about you, Aris," Kael said, his gaze softening. "Your mind, that spirit of discovery and foresight. You're always seeing the next layer, the broader implications."

Aris smiled, her cheeks tinting a faint, shy red. "Thank you, Kael. That means a lot coming from you." Her eyes met his, bright and sincere. "But honestly, nothing I've done, nothing I could do, can compare to your insights into the QED's quantum tunneling anomalies. The way you're mapping the informational decoherence... It's genuinely astounding. You see things others don't, things that feel almost... intractable until you find the elegant pathway."

Kael felt a familiar warmth creep up his neck, his skin darkening. He reached up, rubbing the back of his head self-consciously. Taking compliments had always been a struggle, especially when they felt so deeply earned by someone else. 

"I just... I see the patterns," he mumbled, a bashful grin on his face. Aris leaned back slightly, her gaze sweeping over the bustling restaurant. 

"Have you ever been here before, Kael?" she asked.

He hesitated for a fraction of a second. The truth, that he had barely been anywhere outside his home, was not something he could share. "No," he admitted, a small, genuine smile forming. "But I've always wanted to. Have you?"

Aris's eyes widened. "Me neither\! I didn't even know this place existed until I looked it up. It's wonderful."

A Vigilae servant, its form shimmering with quiet efficiency, gilded silently to their table. 

"Greetings, esteemed diners. Do you know what you wish to order this cycle?" its synthesized voice chimed, polite and friendly.

Kael, unfamiliar with restaurant customs, paused. "Do you have a... a list, or something, of what you have?"

"Yes, sir," the Vigilae replied smoothly, its optical sensors focusing on Kael. It then waved a transparent hand over their tabletop. With a soft hum, a detailed holographic menu shimmered into existence, floating a few centimeters above the table's surface. It displayed an array of vibrant images, names, and descriptions for every meal, drink, and dessert, each accompanied by its price. "Take your time. Simply press the illuminated activation node on your table when you are ready, sir and madam." With another silent glide, the Vigilae retreated to attend to other patrons.

Kael and Aris leaned in, their heads close, as they began to browse the shimmering display. "Look at these names," Aris chuckled, pointing to a particularly elaborate dish. " 'Whispering Dusk Stew'? What even is that?"

"And the options," Kael added, scrolling through pages of beverages and exotic confections. "There are so many." He noticed Aris's soft sigh as she reached the bottom of a page, her gaze lingering on the price indicators.

"Don't worry about the cost," he assured her, a touch of pride in his voice. "My father gave me a little extra Sol to add to my own for tonight."

Aris's smile warmed, her eyes sparkling. "Please tell him thank you for me as well, Kael."

Aris was about to say something, a small smile playing on her lips as she looked over the menu. But Kael's internal alarm bells were screaming. He hadn't complimented her. He'd been so consumed by his own nervousness and the fruit presentation, he'd completely missed it.

He quickly interjected, cutting off her nascent sentence. "Your tunic... It's really beautiful, Aris. And you look incredible tonight."

A deep, vibrant red blush spread across Aris's cheeks, contrasting beautifully with the soft glow of her attire. She looked down at the menu, a bashful smile gracing her face. "Oh. Thank you, Kael," she murmured, her voice a little softer than before.

She lifted her gaze, a genuine warmth in her eyes. "You look very striking as well.. That iridescent synth-silk... it suits you."

Kael felt his own skin warm in response, his ears tinting darker. "Thank you," he managed, looking down at his hands on the table.

A comfortable, yet slightly awkward, silence settled between them, punctuated only by the distant hum of the restaurant. They both seemed to be studying the holographic menu, though neither was truly seeing it. Just as the silence began to stretch, Aris perked up, her natural effervescence returning. She looked at him, her eyes bright with curiosity.

"So," she began, "tell me about your work at the Astronomy Magistrate. What's it like, gazing out into the void from the observatories?"

Kael's bashfulness faded, replaced by the familiar passion for his work. "It's… vast," he began, his voice gaining confidence. 

"There's so much out there. We see objects flying at immense speeds in every direction, tiny points of light, distant echoes. I've tracked so many stellar bodies, distant yellow stars, blazing white dwarfs, even ancient, dying red giants." He paused, his gaze seeming to drift beyond the restaurant walls, into the cosmic expanse. "And the red shift... everything is moving away from us. Every observation confirms it. The universe is just constantly expanding, stretching further and further into the unknown." He realized he was rambling, his usual enthusiasm for the subject taking over. "Sorry," he mumbled, glancing at her, "I tend to go on."

Aris was resting her face in her hands, her elbows on the table, a warm, genuine smile lighting her features. "Don't be," she said, her voice soft with fascination. "It's incredible. I've always wondered about the future, about what's beyond our atmosphere. Do you think we'll ever leave the surface? Really explore space?"

Kael's earlier bashfulness was completely gone, replaced by a profound sense of optimism. "I hope so, Aris. With everything we're learning, especially with what the QEDs are starting to hint at... I truly hope we do."

"And what about your work, Aris?" Kael asked, genuinely curious. "You're in Molecular Biology, aren't you?"

Aris brightened, her enthusiasm palpable. "That's right. I specialize in cross-species manipulation and genetic engineering." She saw his slightly perplexed expression and chuckled. "Think of it this way: everything living has a blueprint, right? DNA, RNA, and all the instructions for how to build and operate an organism. Genetic engineering is like rewriting sections of that blueprint, adding new instructions, or tweaking existing ones to give an organism new traits or abilities."

She leaned forward, her eyes alight. "For example, we might take the incredibly efficient nutrient-scavenging pathways from a deep-strata extremophile lichen and introduce them into a more common surface-dwelling fungal crop. This wouldn't be to make it glow, necessarily, but to give it a much higher yield from nutrient-poor regolith, helping us cultivate food in previously barren zones. That’s a form of cross-species manipulation, taking genetic material from one type of life and integrating it successfully into another, despite their evolutionary differences."

"Or," she continued, "we might engineer specific symbiotic microorganisms to thrive in our atmospheric processors, making them far more efficient at converting atmospheric trace elements into breathable compounds. Or, on a more complex scale, imagine enhancing the natural regenerative capabilities of certain Dorinian fauna, allowing them to regrow lost energy-siphon organs in years instead of decades. My work is about understanding the fundamental biological code and then thoughtfully, precisely, re-engineering it to solve problems, create new resources, or even enhance existing life forms in beneficial ways. It's about designing life, within ethical bounds, to better suit our needs and environment."

Kael listened, completely engrossed. The complexity of her field, the ethical tightrope, and the profound potential all resonated deeply with his own drive for innovation.

"Someday," Aris added, her voice softening with a hint of longing, "I hope to do what your mother has achieved with those fruits, but with extinct animal life. To bring back the lost fauna of Dorin, not just as genetic curiosities, but as living, breathing presences. That's the ultimate dream."

Kael was genuinely impressed. "Aris," he said, his voice imbued with sincerity, "I have no doubt you'll do something truly great one day. That's an incredible ambition."

She smiled, a hint of blush reappearing on her cheeks. "I hope so," she replied. She then glanced at the shimmering holographic menu. "But perhaps, for now, we should order? I'm suddenly quite hungry."

They leaned over the table, making their selections. Kael pointed to a dish. "I'll have the Crimson Spore Bloom, with the spiced regolith-loaf on the side."

Aris, after a moment's consideration, chose next. "And I'll try the Deep-Strata Crustacean Bake, with the lumiflora garnish, please."

Kael tapped the activation node on the table. The Vigilae materialized moments later. "Excellent choices, esteemed diners," it chimed. "Your selections will be prepared promptly. Would you care for any beverages while you wait?"

"Yes\!" Kael and Aris said in unison, then burst into laughter.

"Definitely," Kael added, a nervous chuckle escaping him. "I need something to settle my nerves."

Aris's eyes sparkled. "Oh, Kael, there's no need to be nervous. I'm the same girl you've been talking to over the Ionics for weeks. And this is our third date after all."

"I know," he agreed, his skin deepening a shade. "But in person, you're... a little intimidating."

Aris laughed again, a pure, uninhibited sound. She reached across the table, her hand gently covering his. Her touch was warm and soft against his skin, sending a jolt that was less a nervous flutter, more comforting surge. "I'm just me," she said simply, her gaze steady and kind.

They talked easily then, the conversation flowing in a way Kael hadn't dared to hope, touching on everything and nothing, the awkwardness of the initial embrace dissolving completely.

Shortly, the Vigilae returned, gliding with the familiar grace of its kind, bearing their chosen meals. Kael's Crimson Spore Bloom arrived first. It was a visual feast: a large, almost sentient-looking fungal cap, its surface a deep, velvety red that shimmered with subtle iridescence, perfectly centered on a dark obsidian plate. Steam rose from its core, carrying an earthy, musky scent, underscored by a faint, nutty sweetness. Beside it lay thick slices of regolith-loaf, a dense, dark bread infused with warming spices, emitting a rich, comforting aroma.

Then came Aris's Deep-Strata Crustacean Bake. It was presented within a polished shell of some unknown, pearlescent material, nestled in a bed of delicate, edible crystal filaments. As the Vigilae gently lifted the shell's lid, a wave of briny, savory steam wafted across the table, carrying hints of smoked minerals and a sharp, peppery tang. Within, a tangled mass of segmented, pale orange protein lay, interspersed with vibrant green tendrils of lumiflora garnish that pulsed with a faint, internal light, adding both visual appeal and a fresh, almost herbaceous scent to the rich, oceanic aroma.

The evening unfurled with a natural grace, a comfortable blend of intellectual sparring and genuine connection. Kael and Aris talked and laughed, the unique flavors of their exotic meals blending with the effervescence of the conversation. They spoke of the sprawling possibilities of their respective fields, the mysteries of the vast Dorinian interior, and the mundane joys of everyday life. Drinks were ordered, consumed, and refilled, a soft, warming algae-wine that settled Kael's remaining nerves and brought an easy flush to Aris's cheeks. The hours melted away, marked only by the shifting light of the city beyond the restaurant's panoramic windows.

Then, with a reluctant sigh, Aris checked the time on her internal display. "Oh, Kael," she said, a hint of genuine disappointment in her voice, "as much as I'd love to lose myself in this conversation for another cycle, I have a big day tomorrow. Early start."

But before she could stand, a mischievous grin spread across her face, lighting up her features. Her eyes, twinkling with a knowing glint, fixed on Kael. "You know," she began, her voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper, "when I was younger, my same-age friends and I were playing in a public park, just like any other cycle. And we saw another child there, a boy. We'd never seen him before, didn't even know he existed. We called out to him, told him to come play with us. But he just… ran off, away from us, as fast as he could."

Kael felt a sudden chill, his skin tinting a dark red. He looked away, his mind scrambling for a reply, a memory, anything to mask the sudden surge of unease.

Aris's voice was soft, probing. "Did you ever meet a strange child like that, Kael? Someone you just… couldn't place?"

He forced himself to meet her gaze, a casual shrug he hoped looked convincing. "Me? No. I was always more of an indoor person, preferring a workshop to parks. So, no, I haven't."

Aris's grin widened, a knowing light in her eyes that sent a shiver down his spine. "Okay, okay," she said, pushing back her chair. "Just, if you ever think of anything, anything at all, you want to tell me. Know that I'll pass no judgment, and I can definitely keep a secret."

She stood, and Kael rose too, his movements stiff. She leaned in, a soft brush of her lips against his cheek, a warm whisper of bio-luminescent flora. As she turned to leave, Kael's gaze fell to the basket on the table.

"Aris, wait\!" he called, picking it up. "You forgot this." He followed her out the restaurant door to the illuminated street.

She turned, her eyes widening in surprise. "Oh\! That's mine?\! Thank you, K. My family will be so excited."

Then, without warning, she leaned in again. This time, her lips found his, warm and soft. It was a slow, gentle kiss that stretched, holding them in a bubble away from the bustling city. Kael's heart hammered against his ribs, every nerve alive to her touch. The moment stretched, timeless and perfect, until the approach of a public transit unit, its soft bell chiming its arrival, broke the spell.

"Is one of you getting on?" the transit AI inquired politely.

Aris pulled back, a radiant smile on her face. "Goodbye, K," she whispered, her voice laced with a promise. "Talk to you tomorrow."

He watched her board the transport, his gaze fixed on her receding form until the glowing unit was just a distant point of light, swallowed by the city's brilliance. He stood there for a long moment, the warmth of her kiss still lingering on his lips. A slow, huge smile spread across his face, encompassing his entire being.

"Woo hoo\!" he let out, a burst of unadulterated joy into the Dorinian night. He sank onto a nearby public bench, a contented sigh escaping him, lost in the memory of the evening as he waited for the next transit unit heading toward his quiet corner of town.

The transit unit deposited Kael back in his quiet residential sector, a stark contrast to the vibrant Social District he'd just left. The city here hummed with a softer, almost reverent glow. He entered their quarters, the automatic door sliding shut with a barely audible sigh. Inside, the usual household hum was muted. His parents' torpor chambers were dark, and Rixie's core processor was likely in a low-power state. It was still hours before the sun would touch the horizon, a time when most Dorinians sought their deepest restorative rest.

Kael, however, was far from resting. He floated on an almost physical wave of elation. The nervous tension that had gripped him earlier was completely gone, replaced by a lightness he hadn't known he could feel. He "glided" on this feeling, almost effortlessly ascending the stairs to his own torpor chamber. 

The chamber hummed, inviting him into its cool, dark embrace. He lay down, the automated systems adjusting to his form, preparing for the deep metabolic slowdown of torpor.
